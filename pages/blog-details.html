Color Grading for Autumn Editorials

By Julian Rossi

Achieving a cohesive look across a 20-image editorial spread shot in varying light conditions is the true test of a colorist's skill.

The Challenge

When the brief arrived for the Autumn '26 cover story, the requirements were clear: deep, moody tones, rich ochres, and desaturated greens, all while keeping the skin tones luminous and natural.

The Approach

We began by establishing a primary LUT that handled the heavy lifting of the environment colors. We targeted the lush greens of the outdoor location, shifting their hue towards warmer, earthy tones and pulling down their saturation. This immediately gave the images an autumnal feel.

"The secret to a cinematic grade is not just what colors you add, but what colors you take away."

However, applying a global grade often destroys skin tones. We utilized luminosity masks and advanced blending modes in Capture One to isolate the subject, applying a completely different treatment to the skin to maintain its natural warmth and vitality against the moody background.

Julian Rossi

Lead Retoucher & Colorist
